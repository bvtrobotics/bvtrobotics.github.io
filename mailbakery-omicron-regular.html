Welcome Back!
This week marks the reformation of the robotics club after the summer break with our first meeting on Tuesday, September 3. This meeting was used to introduce the robotics club to new students who were interested in becoming a part of our team. We spoke about aspects of the club and changes that were being made to the club from last year. On Thursday, September 5, we met in the engineering shop to teach underclassmen about clawbots and basic vex concepts. We showed the underclassmen how vex robots work and how to control them.

First, Vex, Fundraising, and Outreach
Announcements
Vex Robotics

Vex is going through big changes from last year with the focus of vex being shifted towards introducing newer members to the club with the more experienced members of the club mentoring the teams we have in a more orderly setup than last year.

First Robotics

First Robotics will now be in effect throughout the year. We are shifting more focus to our first team than prior years to help better prepare student for the build season in January. We are now going to more teach students throughout the year how to drive, build, program, and design the first robot.

Outreach

Outreach is the part of the team that reaches out to the community and organization in order to spread the importance of STEM and to support their efforts Over the summer we started visiting Libraries and holding events where children can learn about stem and robotics at an early age.

Fundraising

Lastly, fundraising is an important part of our team and can allow us to accomplish many more feats than we would be able to otherwise. We will be bringing back meat sticks and encourage all members of the club to help sell these to support the team.
Mission Statement
To order to positively influence and inspire our community, peers, and younger students, as well as teach them about the impact their work could have on the future, BVT Robotics plans to spread our knowledge about science, engineering, and technology to through exciting mentor-based programs and foster well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, interpersonal communication skills, and leadership development.
