Welcome Back!
This week we are continuing to work on preparation for the First Competition through inspecting past robots, working with our community, and honing our programming skills. Inspecting past first robots allows for inexperienced members to learn about the features of the robots and processes the robot goes through to complete its tasks in a competition. In our community, we are teaching children about the importance of stem by providing activities that show stem concepts in a way they can understand. Programming has added an online feature to the clubs and allows for the members to learn and add to the programming of the First robot without needing to attend meetings.

First, Vex, Fundraising, and Outreach
Announcements

First Robotics

We are continuing our work on reverse engineering our First Robot. We started by taking pictures of the First Robot and scheduling parts and how they were previously fit together. We have shown the new members of the team the basics of robotic assembly and documenting. The reversing engineering team has learned about the physical and pneumatic components of the robot that allow it to properly function.

Outreach

We are now mentoring vex IQ teams in Douglas on Tuesdays and Fridays. We also are planning to host a STEM event in Uxbridge, Milbury, and Grafton. Chairman’s meetings will be starting on Wednesdays after school and will be discussing a plan of action for the chairman’s award. The Chairman's Award is awarded to the team who makes the largest impact to the first Community, their community and beyond.

Mission Statement
To order to positively influence and inspire our community, peers, and younger students, as well as teach them about the impact their work could have on the future, BVT Robotics plans to spread our knowledge about science, engineering, and technology to through exciting mentor-based programs and foster well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, interpersonal communication skills, and leadership development.
